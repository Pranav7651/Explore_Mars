Facts about Mars

1) Red Planet: Mars is often called the "Red Planet" due to its reddish
appearance, which is caused by iron oxide, or rust, on its surface.

2) Similarities to Earth: Mars shares some similarities with Earth,
such as its rotation period (about 24.6 hours) and the presence of
polar ice caps. These polar ice caps consist of water ice and carbon
dioxide ice (dry ice).

3) Mount Olympus: Mars hosts the largest volcano in the solar
system, Olympus Mons. It stands at about 13.6 miles (22 kilometers)
high, making it nearly three times taller than Mount Everest, Earth's
tallest mountain.
